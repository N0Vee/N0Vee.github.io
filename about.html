In 1883, tailor August Stenströms realized his dream and opened a shop in the heart of Helsingborg. His knowledge of how to sew shirts he had learned from his many trips to Berlin and now he took this expertise with him to convince the Swedes about fabric, fit and feel in the perfect shirt. The rumor spread quickly that the Tailor in Helsingborg made them the best shirts and in 1899 Stenström's shirt factory was started and success was soon a fact. At the same time, the store was sold to merchant Knut Håkansson, who continued to run the store with Stenström's shirts as the most important product group. The store, Stenströms Herr & Damekipering, is located today at Stortorget 12, Helsingborg, Sweden and has done so since 1906. Today, the store is run by the Anderberg family, who are the fifth family to carry on the legacy of August Stenströms.
